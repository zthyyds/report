Angle 1：Customer-Complaint True-North View Needs Reinforcement

Redefinition stance: Reason backward from "no customer complaints": customer complaints are the only true north; all node/72-hour/distance rules should submit to zero-tolerance for complaints

Current logic

Current rules center on the 72-hour deadline, distance tiering, and rigid communication nodes, with complaints only as a baseline constraint. This view flips it: the ultimate purpose of every action is zero complaints; nodes and the 72-hour deadline are merely means.

Proposition (redefinition)

The only true north of mis-sort control is zero customer complaints. The 72-hour deadline and distance rules that do not reduce complaints are invalid rules and should be restructured.

Counter-questions (10)

  1. Has the 72-hour deadline reduced complaints, quantitatively?
  2. How many complaints arose from in-timeframe non-rescue?
  3. The correlation between distance tiering and complaint rate
  4. Did rigid node execution lower complaints?
  5. After SSS error code supplement, did complaints drop to zero?
  6. Did complaints drop after face-to-face talks with key personnel?
  7. Did complaints drop after bonus deductions, or did outlets give up?
  8. Once a complaint is established, whose retroactive deduction is truly effective?
  9. Does the customer care more about timeliness or attitude?
  10. Is zero-tolerance for complaints too costly?

Evidence (business basis)

The logic section repeatedly stresses "on the premise of no customer complaints", indicating complaints are the highest constraint. Yet all rules revolve around 72-hour/distance/node, with no causal quantification between complaint rate and each rule.
